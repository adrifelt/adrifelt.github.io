About Me

I'm a software engineer at Google, on the Chrome security team. My goal is to make security an intuitive, helpful experience for non-experts. I'm working on improving the user experience of HTTPS and web permissions. You can find my current job title etc. on LinkedIn.

I'm a recovering academic who tries to blend research and engineering. Previously, I was a research scientist on the Google security research team. I received a PhD in CS from UC Berkeley, where I did my dissertation on permission models. I still publish (albeit less often) and sit on program committees (e.g., USENIX 2013-2015, SOUPS 2015, Oakland 2016).

Contact

"I found a security bug in Chrome." Awesome! You should file it in the Chromium bug tracker to get credit. Here are instructions on how to file a good security bug report. If you think I should specifically look at it, ask for me in the comments and someone can cc me.

"Can you send me a copy of Stowaway?" Stowaway is extremely out of date and will not produce accurate results for a corpus of modern Android apps. You should use PScout instead, which has been updated to handle modern apps. If you still want to see Stowaway for some reason, e-mail me and I'll share a copy with you.
